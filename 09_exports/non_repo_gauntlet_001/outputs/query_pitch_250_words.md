# Query Pitch (250 words)

In a colony of identical black-and-white penguins, Pip stands out — not because she wants to, but because she's covered in bright polka dots. Teased and lonely, she sets out across the Southern Ocean to find a place where she belongs. Her journey takes her through the magnificent Twilight Zone (beautiful and full of lights, not scary at all), where she befriends Wally the Walrus (who forgets everything but never forgets a friend), matches wits with the mischievous Squid Sisters, and follows the mysterious Glimmer — a bioluminescent fish who appears at every turning point. Along the way, Pip discovers that the thing making her different is the very thing that helps her save her new friends and find her way home. THE ADVENTURES OF PIP THE POLKA-DOT PENGUIN is a humorous and heartfelt picture book (ages 3–7, ~650 words) that combines the emotional depth of a classic belonging story with the playful energy of a comic adventure. It's designed as a standalone with series potential — Pip's world has more oceans to explore and more friends to meet.
