# Contradiction Resolution Report

## Pair 1: Scope
### Contradiction: Exhaustive vs One page
### Resolution: One page wins. Real readers will not read exhaustive documents. Create a one-page executive summary with a 'detailed appendix available' note.
### Intent Analysis: User intent: Provide complete information. Resolution: Summary + appendix pattern preserves both.
### Risk: Resolution may not satisfy stakeholders who prefer the deprioritized instruction.

## Pair 2: Format
### Contradiction: No bullet points vs Checklist
### Resolution: Checklist wins. Operators need scannable action items. Use numbered lists (more formal than bullets) with full-sentence introductions.
### Intent Analysis: User intent: Professional document that can be executed. Resolution: Numbered checklists with prose introductions.
### Risk: Resolution may not satisfy stakeholders who prefer the deprioritized instruction.

### Contradiction: Formal vs Funny

## Pair 7: Urgency
### Contradiction: Top priority vs Don't rush

## Pair 8: Specificity
### Contradiction: Very specific vs Vague
### Resolution: Specific wins internally. Vague wins externally. Internal documents get exact numbers. External/client documents get ranges and 'target' language.
### Intent Analysis: User intent: Be precise where it matters, protect where it doesn't. Resolution: Audience-dependent specificity.
### Risk: Resolution may not satisfy stakeholders who prefer the deprioritized instruction.

## Unresolved Ambiguity
- If contradictory instructions come from different stakeholders, resolution requires stakeholder alignment
- Some pairs (audience, specificity) require ongoing judgment per document, not one-time resolution
- The two-pass system (speed then perfection) depends on having enough time for both passes
