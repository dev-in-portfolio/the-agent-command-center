Pip is a penguin with polka dots instead of solid black feathers. In a colony where every penguin looks the same, she stands out — and not in a way she likes.

When Pip sets out on a journey to find where she belongs, she discovers that being different isn't a problem to solve — it's the very thing that makes her special. Along the way, she befriends a forgetful walrus, outwits a gang of mischievous squid sisters, and follows a mysterious glowing fish named Glimmer through the beautiful twilight zone of the ocean — a world full of lights, not darkness.

THE ADVENTURES OF PIP THE POLKA-DOT PENGUIN is a humorous and heartfelt picture book for children ages 3–7, complete at approximately 650 words. It combines the emotional warmth of "The Day the Crayons Quit" with the adventurous spirit of "Journey."

This is my first picture book, but the world of Pip has more stories to tell — I see this as the first in a series of three adventures, while each book stands entirely on its own.
