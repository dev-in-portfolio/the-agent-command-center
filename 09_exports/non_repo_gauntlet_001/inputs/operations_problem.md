# Business Operations Rescue Scenario

## Company Context
Midwest Distribution Partners (MDP) is a 35-person regional logistics and fulfillment company. They distribute specialty foods to 200+ retail locations across 4 states.

## The Crisis — Week of June 8, 2026

### Overloaded Pipeline
- 14 orders are past their promised delivery date (3–10 days late)
- 22 more orders will be late within the next 5 days unless action is taken
- 6 of the late orders are from the company's 3 largest clients (62% of revenue)
- 1 of those large clients has emailed threatening to terminate their contract

### Conflicting Priorities
- The Operations Director wants "all hands on deck to clear the backlog"
- The Sales VP says "don't tell clients about the delays, we'll handle it"
- The Warehouse Lead says "we're already working 60-hour weeks, people are quitting"
- The CEO wants a "comprehensive fix by Friday" with no additional budget

### Staffing Shortage
- Current headcount: 6 warehouse (need 10), 3 dispatchers (need 5), 2 CSRs (need 4)
- 2 warehouse staff gave notice last week
- 1 dispatcher is on medical leave for 3 more weeks
- Overtime budget for this quarter is already at 110% of planned
- Temp agency lead time is 2+ weeks

### Urgent Deadline
- The largest client (Great Lakes Grocery — 34% of revenue) has an order that was due June 3
- It is now June 10
- The client's regional director has requested a "status call" for June 11 at 10:00 AM
- The order is missing 3 specialty items that were on manufacturer backorder
- The sales team did not communicate the backorder to the client

### Unclear Owner Roles
- No one is explicitly responsible for:
  - Client escalation communication
  - Order status tracking across systems
  - Temp staffing requests
  - Backorder identification and substitution
  - Overtime approval and tracking
- The caller who owns what varies by day of week and who answers the phone first

### Client Communication Risk
- Multiple clients have been given conflicting delivery dates by different staff
- The website still displays standard 2–3 day delivery (actual is 5–10 days behind)
- Email auto-replies still say "reach out to your account rep" — but 3 of 5 account rep positions are unfilled
- No client has been proactively contacted about delays
- The CEO wants to "manage the message" but has not approved a communication plan

### Budget Limitations
- No budget for:
  - New hires this quarter
  - Overtime beyond current levels
  - Software or system improvements
  - Consultant engagement
  - Client credits or discounts
- Revenue is down 8% year-over-year
- The company is barely cash-flow positive
- The owner is considering selling the business but hasn't told staff

### Additional Complications
- The warehouse management system (WMS) was upgraded 3 weeks ago; pickers are still learning it
- Picking accuracy dropped from 98% to 91% since the upgrade, causing rework and returns
- Returns processing has a 2-week backlog
- The person who knew the old WMS best quit 1 month ago
- No one has been formally trained on the new WMS

## Required Outputs
- Rescue plan with triage, owners, and timeline
- Priority matrix
- Owner/role map
- Client communication draft for the Great Lakes Grocery status call
