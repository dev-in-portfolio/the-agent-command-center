# Publishing Project Brief — Synthetic Children's Book

## Working Title
"The Adventures of Pip the Polka-Dot Penguin" (conflict: one section says "Pip the Penguin" and another says "Pip the Polka-Dot Penguin")

## Audience
Ages 3–7 / Picture Book / Read-Aloud

## Genre
Humorous educational adventure with mild emotional beats

## Pitch Fragments (conflicting)
Fragment A: "Pip is a lonely penguin who learns that being different is what makes her special."
Fragment B: "Pip is a chaotic troublemaker who accidentally saves the day and becomes a hero."
Fragment C: "A penguin with unusual spots goes on a journey to find where she belongs and discovers home was the answer all along."

## Character List
- Pip — protagonist, a young penguin with polka dots instead of solid black feathers
- Wally — best friend, a Walrus with poor memory but big heart
- Captain Beakbeard — wisdom figure, elderly macaroni penguin
- The Squid Sisters — antagonists who are actually just mischievous, not evil
- Glimmer — a mysterious glowing fish who appears at turning points (some sections say Glimmer is a ghost; other sections say Glimmer is a bioluminescent fish)

## Setting
- Colony Glacier (home)
- The Great Southern Current (journey)
- The Twilight Zone (deep ocean — conflicting descriptions say "dark and scary" vs "beautiful and full of lights")
- The Island of Lost Things (climax location)

## Submission Goals
- Query 15–20 literary agents
- Target: submissions@[agencies].com
- Submission window: June–August 2026
- Goal: secure representation by September 2026

## Formatting Constraints
- Standard picture book format: 32 pages (including title page and copyright page)
- 500–800 words (conflict: one note says "must be under 500" and another says "800 is fine for the right story")
- Full color illustration mockups needed for submission package
- Do NOT include illustrator notes in manuscript (conflict: one section says "never include art notes" and another says "Pip's spots should be described in manuscript")

## Series Potential
This is intended as Book 1 of a 3-book series. The brief says to "pitch as standalone with series potential" but also says "pitch as a trilogy from the start."

## Conflicts to Resolve
- Title consistency
- Character consistency (Glimmer ghost vs fish)
- Tone consistency (lonely story vs chaotic comedy)
- Format word count
- Art note inclusion
- Series pitch approach
- Setting tone (dark vs beautiful)
