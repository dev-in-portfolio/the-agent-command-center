# Demo Success Scorecard

## Scoring Rubric
Rate each dimension from 1 (low) to 5 (high).

### Stakeholder Confidence
- 1: Reviewers do not understand or trust the system
- 2: Partial understanding, significant concerns
- 3: General understanding, minor concerns
- 4: Clear understanding, few concerns
- 5: Strong confidence, ready for next phase

### Clarity
- 1: Presentation was confusing
- 2: Key points were unclear
- 3: Most points were clear
- 4: Clear and well-structured
- 5: Crystal clear, no questions left unanswered

### Safety Confidence
- 1: Reviewers concerned about safety
- 2: Safety posture not fully understood
- 3: Safety posture understood
- 4: Safety posture trusted
- 5: Safety posture strongly trusted

### Demo Readiness
- 1: Technical issues during demo
- 2: Some issues, workable
- 3: Smooth but minor issues
- 4: Smooth demo
- 5: Flawless demo experience

### Runtime Activation Readiness
- 1: Nowhere near ready
- 2: Architecture needs work
- 3: Architecture ready, planning needed
- 4: Ready to begin planning
- 5: Ready to activate (note: this should not happen — runtime activation is a separate phase)

## Next-Step Recommendation
Based on scores:
- Average 4+: Proceed to runtime activation planning
- Average 3-4: Address specific concerns, schedule follow-up
- Average below 3: Major rework needed before proceeding

## Notes
- Low runtime activation readiness scores are expected and correct (it has not started)
- High safety confidence is critical
- Demo readiness reflects presenter preparation, not system quality
